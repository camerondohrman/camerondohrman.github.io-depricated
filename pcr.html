Ethics Training

- Summer 2023
- 3 Points Ethics and Professionalism
As part of the onboarding process for Costco supervisors we take a four hour training course titled "Drawing the Line" which goes over a variety of ethically questionable scenarios and how they can be dealt with in a human and professional manner. The solutions are all common sense, but it is still important to spell it out so that ethical behavior is second nature.

Romeo and Jules

- Spring 2019
- 5 Points AEID
Romeo and Jules was a mainstage play put on by the University of Washington while I was a student there. In collaboration with the Trans* advocacy groups on campus, the School of Drama wrote a script following the plot of Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet but including several non-binary characters and conflicts unique to their identity. I played the role of Tybalt, which required a new level of emotional intelligence to do justice to the material. It is the single performance I am most proud of.

MGMT 311

- Fall 2023
- 3 Points Professional Communication
This course on organizational behavior all led up to a class presentation pitching a new business idea. My idea was a medieval themed family friendly restaurant with a bar and arcade. Not especially original, but the entertaining part was that the management of this fictional company was organized like a feudal dutchy, complete with a Chancellor, Spymaster, Marshal, and Chaplain. We brought costumes and a lot of dark age humor to the presentation. We had the class rolling in laughter, and got straight A's as a bonus.

The above is all a true account of my exploits.
